Why you should choose us?

Insurance provides a safety net to protect your financial well-being in the event of unexpected and potentially costly events. It covers the costs associated with accidents, illnesses, property damage, or other unforeseen circumstances, preventing you from experiencing a significant financial burden.In many places, specific types of insurance, such as auto insurance, are legally required. Compliance with these legal insurance requirements is essential to avoid fines, legal penalties, or even the loss of driving privileges. Insurance helps protect valuable assets, such as homes, cars, or businesses. In the event of a disaster, theft, or other covered incident, insurance can provide compensation for the loss or damage to these assets.

The history

Ancient Civilizations: The earliest forms of insurance were practiced by ancient civilizations. In Babylon, around 1750 BC, the Code of Hammurabi contained provisions for merchants who engaged in trade and shipping, allowing them to pay an additional sum to protect their goods from losses due to theft or damage.

Ancient China and India: Historical records suggest that early forms of insurance existed in ancient China and India as well. In China, merchants and traders established mutual aid societies to protect themselves from financial losses. In India, the Maurya Empire (322-185 BC) and later dynasties had systems in place to compensate individuals for losses related to shipping and trade.

Ancient Greece and Rome: Ancient Greeks and Romans also engaged in practices resembling insurance. They formed burial societies to help families cover funeral expenses and provided protection against the risks of sea voyages.

Medieval and Renaissance Europe: medieval Europe, various forms of insurance began to emerge. Merchants in cities like Genoa and Venice established "bottomry" contracts, which allowed them to borrow money for their trade voyages, pledging their ships as collateral. If the voyage was successful, the debt was repaid with interest; if the ship was lost, the debt was forgiven. These contracts laid the foundation for modern marine insurance.

Insurance Companies and Regulation: As trade and commerce expanded in the 18th and 19th centuries, insurance companies began to form and offer various types of coverage, including life insurance, property insurance, and liability insurance. Governments also started regulating the insurance industry to protect consumers and ensure the solvency of insurers.
